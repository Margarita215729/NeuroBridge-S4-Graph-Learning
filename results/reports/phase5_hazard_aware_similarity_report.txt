NeuroBridge-S4 Graph Learning — Phase 5 Report
Hazard-Aware Graph Embeddings and Similarity Mapping
================================================================

NeuroBridge-S4 connects individual biological adaptation patterns to NASA's five human spaceflight hazard categories without claiming actual exposure, diagnosis, or causal proof.

Overview
----------------------------------------------------------------
Phase 5 maps each biological adaptation graph into a hazard-aware graph-feature
space and adds NASA HRP five-hazard context relevance scores. It does not assign
clinical labels. It maps biological adaptation graphs into a hazard-aware feature
space so that reviewers can inspect structural similarity and HRP hazard-context
relevance.

Domain coverage limitations
----------------------------------------------------------------
  Space Radiation: 4/5 domains (80%).
  Isolation and Confinement: 1/6 domains (17%).
  Distance from Earth: 1/5 domains (20%).
  Gravity Fields: 5/6 domains (83%).
  Hostile / Closed Environments: 3/6 domains (50%).
  The current NHANES-derived proxy has limited coverage for isolation/confinement
  and distance-from-Earth autonomy contexts. Additional Artemis II data streams
  (sleep/circadian, autonomic, emotional, cognitive-load) would strengthen coverage.

Interpretation guardrail
----------------------------------------------------------------
  Hazard relevance is not exposure measurement, risk scoring, diagnosis, or causal
  attribution. Similarity is structural, not clinical. PCA is a visualization, not a
  classifier. No clinical or psychological conditions are inferred.
