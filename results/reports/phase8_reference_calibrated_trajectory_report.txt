OVERVIEW
------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Phase 8 keeps within-subject self-baseline tracking as the primary method and adds a reference-calibrated variability envelope. It estimates whether each subject's own baseline-relative graph change is small, moderate, or unusually large relative to expected variability.

WHAT THE ENVELOPE IS
------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  The reference envelope does not define whether a person is healthy or unhealthy. It calibrates how large a within-subject graph change is relative to expected variability in available proxy or analog data.

WHAT THE ENVELOPE IS NOT
------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  - It is not a diagnosis or abnormality label.
  - It is not a health risk score.
  - It is not treatment guidance.
  - It is not an exposure measurement.
  - It does not define a healthy-vs-unhealthy endpoint.

LIMITATIONS
------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  - Envelope quality depends entirely on reference/analog data quality.
  - Example envelopes are schema demonstration only and are not evidence.
  - Envelope exceedance is descriptive, not a clinical threshold.
  - The envelope does not validate clinical or operational thresholds.
  - Domain coverage limitations affect which deltas can be scored.

HOW THIS SUPPORTS HRP REVIEW
------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  Astronauts may show operationally meaningful within-subject shifts that still sit inside population-normal ranges. A reference-calibrated envelope helps reviewers avoid overreacting to expected variability while flagging unusually large self-baseline shifts for closer expert review — useful when crews are too small for group inference.

  Outside-envelope does not mean disease, danger, or health risk. It means the baseline-relative graph change is larger than expected under the current calibration envelope and may deserve expert review.
